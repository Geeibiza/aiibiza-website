Section 01 — The Problem

The Ruling That Changed Everything

In February 2026, a Southern District of New York judge issued a ruling that every law firm's technology committee needs to read. It is not hypothetical. It is precedent.

Federal Ruling — SDNY, February 2026

United States v. Heppner

The court held that transmitting privileged client communications to a third-party cloud AI provider for processing constitutes a voluntary disclosure to a third party, thereby destroying attorney-client privilege for those communications.

S.D.N.Y., Feb. 2026 — on record as the first federal ruling establishing privilege waiver liability for cloud AI usage in legal practice.

What This Means

Every time a lawyer at your firm uses ChatGPT, Harvey AI, Microsoft Copilot, or any other cloud-based AI tool with client data:

  • Client communications leave your building
  • Data is processed on third-party servers
  • Privilege may be considered waived
  • Evidence becomes discoverable
  • Malpractice exposure increases substantially

Who Is Affected

This ruling applies to any legal practice using cloud AI for:

  • — Legal research (Westlaw AI, Lexis AI)
  • — Contract review and drafting
  • — Document summarization
  • — Client intake and case notes
  • — Deposition preparation
  • — Any AI tool using external servers
The uncomfortable truth: If your lawyers are currently using any cloud AI — even with "enterprise agreements" or "data processing addendums" — you are transmitting client data to a third party. Heppner is now on record. Opposing counsel knows this too.

Section 03 — The Solution

Why Private AI Is the Only Compliant Architecture

Private AI means the model runs on hardware inside your building. No data is transmitted anywhere. No internet connection is required for inference. Your clients' communications never leave your physical control.

The core principle: When AI runs on hardware you own, inside premises you control, processing data that never leaves your building — there is no third-party disclosure. There is no privilege waiver. There is no vendor dependency. There is no per-query cost. You own the system entirely.

What Private AI Protects

  • Attorney-client privilege — data never transmitted to third party
  • Work product doctrine — strategy and analysis stays in-house
  • GDPR compliance — no cross-border data transfers
  • Professional conduct rules — Model Rules 1.6, 1.9 compliance
  • Malpractice insurance — eliminates novel AI liability exposure
  • Client trust — you can actually tell clients their data is safe

Technical Architecture

  • — Hardware delivered and installed in your office
  • — AI model runs entirely on local GPU/CPU
  • — Network-isolated inference (air-gap capable)
  • — Custom-trained on legal document types
  • — Web interface accessible only on your LAN
  • — No external API calls during use
  • — Audit logs stored locally, under your control
